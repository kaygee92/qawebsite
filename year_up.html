What is Year Up

Year Up is a one year program that teaches young adults between the ages of 18-24 technical skills that will help their career. The program is divided into two portions, which are the Learning and Development stage and the Internships stages. The Learning and Development stage trainees learn different skills that will benefit the trainee can take with them at any workplace. The trainees earn an internship after six months, where they are matched with different partners of Year Up. The internships give the trainees hands on experience in various fields using the skills that they have learned.

There are 18 locations all over the United States. All locations have their own special bonds, yet they all have the sense of community that supports the trainee during and after the process. At Year Up, trainees earn 25 college credits and many of the technical skills that will allow them to be successful at the many internships that they can earn. The trainees are assigned advisors and have many different services to help the trainee in any way that they may need in addition to any tutoring that the trainees need.

There are two locations in the Bay Area, the Silicon Valley location and the San Francisco location. The San Francisco location accepts about 120 trainees and the Silicon Valley location has about 80 trainees twice a year. The spring cohort starts in March and the fall cohort starts in September. The trainees learn how to network in the workplace and knowledge from CEOs and employees during Fridays Guest Speakers. The trainee gather more information about how to dress and have workshops on how to react when they are presented with certain situations in the workplace. For more information on the San Francisco location, please email sfbayadmission@yearup.org
